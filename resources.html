Where to Find Past Questions & Textbooks (Legally)

Past questions and textbooks are among the most valuable study assets for Nigerian students. They reveal exam patterns, common question words, and emphasis areas. Use the sources below and rely on department-issued materials for the most relevant practice.

Departmental Library & Course Portal

Most departments keep past questions, seminar reports, project abstracts, and thesis in their library or on an internal portal. Visit the library and request copies of past questions for your specific course codes. If your faculty has an e-portal or LMS, download lecture slides and sample tests from the course pages.

Seniors & Student Associations

Seniors often keep clean collections of past questions and notes. Join your departmental WhatsApp or Telegram groups and ask politely for past questions, then verify their origin. When seniors lend scanned notes, store them in your Drive for easy access.

University Digital Library & Open Access Repositories

Many universities provide e-journals and digital repository access for registered students. Use your university library portal to search textbooks and journals. Open-access sites like Google Scholar, DOAJ, and institutional repositories are excellent for background reading and research references.

Legitimate Online Marketplaces

Sites that sell past questions and notes may be convenient but verify the sellerâ€™s credibility. Prefer materials shared by recognized student associations, departmental pages, or official university outlets to avoid incorrect or incomplete question sets.

How to Use Past Questions Effectively

Collecting past questions is not enough. You must practice them strategically to turn them into higher scores. Below is a tested method used by top students across Nigerian universities.

  1. Collect and organize: For each course, gather at least 5 years of past questions. Label files by year and course code and store them in folders (Drive or local).
  2. Topic mapping: Read the course outline and map questions to topics. This helps you identify which topics are frequently tested.
  3. Timed practice: Simulate exam conditionsâ€”time yourself, sit in a quiet place, and avoid notes during the run. Mark strictly and record missed items.
  4. Error log: Keep an errors spreadsheet. For every wrong answer note the topic, your mistake, and an action (re-read chapter, ask lecturer, practice more).
  5. Progress check: Re-attempt past papers after 1 week and 3 weeks to ensure the knowledge sticks (spaced repetition).
Practical result: Students who practice past questions consistently report faster question recognition, better time management in exams, and improved final scores.

Organize Your Study Materials â€” A Step-by-Step System

Organization saves time. Follow this simple workflow:

  1. Create a master folder (Drive or local) with subfolders for each course code.
  2. Store lecture notes, slides and readings in the course folder and name files by date and topic (e.g., MTH101_Week3_Integration.pdf).
  3. Keep a question folder with past papers and model answers organized by year.
  4. Maintain a revision index (one page per course) listing high-yield topics and the locations of notes and videos.

This system reduces wasted time and ensures you always find the right materials when preparing for a test.

Copyright & Ethical Use

Respect intellectual property. Do not upload or share paid textbooks illegally. Use open educational resources, borrow books from the library, and credit authors in assignments. When obtaining past questions from seniors, confirm their authenticity with the department or library.
